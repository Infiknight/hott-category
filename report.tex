The aim of the project was to implement the foundational types and records occurring in basic category theory as outlined in chapter 9 of the Homotopy Type Theory book.

We defined the following notions:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item category and precategory (in the file \ttt{Category.agda})
  \item isomorphisms in a category, the type \ttt{$a \cong b$} of all isomorphic arrows from $a$ to $b$ (in \ttt{Category.agda})
  \item functors - composition of functors, unit laws and associativity (in \ttt{Functor.agda})
  \item natural transformations - composition, unit laws and associativity \\(in \ttt{Natural-transformation.agda})
  \item definition of adjoints ( in \ttt{Adjoints.agda})
  \item left and right whiskering - composition of a functor with a natural transformation (in \ttt{Natural-transformation.agda})
  \item proof of Lemma 9.1.3 - for arbitrary arrow $f$ in a category $\mathcal{C}$, \ttt{is-iso(f)} is a mere proposition and $a \cong b$ is a set (has homotopy level 0) (the proof is split in \ttt{Category.agda} and \ttt{Lemmas.ttt})
  \item lemma 9.1.4 - \ttt{idtoiso} - in \ttt{Category.agda}
  \item lemma 9.1.8 - the type of objects in an arbitrary category has h-level 1 - and lemma 9.1.9 - in \ttt{Lemmas.agda} 
  \item construction of the precategory $\mathit{Set}$ of all sets ( of h-level 0) and functions between them - in (\ttt{Category.agda})
  \item construction of the functor precategory - the objects are functors between fixed precategories $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$, the morphisms are natural transformations (in \ttt{Natural-transformation.agda})
  \item opposite and product (pre)categories - in \ttt{Categories.agda}
  \item the hom functor $Hom_A : A^{op} \times A \rightarrow Set$, which maps a pair of objects (a, b) to $hom_A(a, b)$ and maps a pair of arrows $(f , f') : Hom_{A^{op} \times A} ((a, b), (a', b'))$ to the function which maps g in $hom_A(a, b)$ to $f' \circ g \circ f$ - in \ttt{Functor.agda}
\end{enumerate}
A natural extension of the current work would be implementation of lemmas 9.5.3 and 9.5.4 (Yoneda lemma).
